በኢትዮጵያ ኦርቶዶክስ ተዋሕዶ ቤተ ክርስቲያን ሰርግ ለማቀድ የመጀመሪያው እርምጃ የደብሩን ቄስ ማግኘት ነው። ካህኑ የመጀመሪያ መመሪያ ይሰጣል፣ ያሉትን ቀኖች ይወያያል እና ቤተክርስቲያኑ ለሠርጉ የሚያስፈልጉትን ነገሮች ያብራራል። ከብዙ ወራት በፊት ለመድረስ ይመከራል. የቤተክርስቲያን የጋብቻ ትምህርት ቤት ኮርስ ለመውሰድ ሊንኩን እንድትጫኑ አጥብቀን እንመክራለን !!

ደረጃ 2፡ አስፈላጊ ሰነዶችን አዘጋጅ
በተለምዶ የሚከተሉትን ያካትታሉ አስፈላጊ ሰነዶችን ይሰብስቡ እና ያዘጋጁ:
  • ለሙሽሪት እና ለሙሽሪት የጥምቀት የምስክር ወረቀቶች።
  • የኢትዮጵያ ኦርቶዶክስ ተዋሕዶ ቤተ ክርስቲያን አባልነት ማረጋገጫ
  • እንደ ፓስፖርት ወይም የልደት የምስክር ወረቀት ያሉ የማንነት ሰነዶች።
እነዚህ ሰነዶች ጥንዶቹ በቤተ ክርስቲያን ውስጥ ለመጋባት ብቁ መሆናቸውን ለማረጋገጥ አስፈላጊ ናቸው።

Wedding Program Services - Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church

Step 1: Contact the representative Priest
The first step in planning a wedding in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church is to contact the parish priest. The priest will provide initial guidance, discuss available dates, and explain the church’s requirements for the wedding. It is recommended to reach out several months in advance.

Step 2: Prepare Required Documentation
Gather and prepare the necessary documents, which typically include:
  • Baptismal certificates for both the bride and groom.
  • Proof of membership in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church.
  • Identity documents such as passports or birth certificates.
These documents are essential for verifying the couple’s eligibility to marry within the church.

Step 3: Attend Pre-Marital Counseling
The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church requires couples to attend pre-marital counseling sessions. These sessions are designed to help the couple understand the spiritual, emotional, and practical aspects of marriage. Schedule these sessions with the parish priest or an appointed counselor.

we strictly recommend you click the link to take the Marriage School course!

Step 4: Choose Wedding Sponsors
Select godparents or sponsors, who are respected members of the church, to support the couple in their married life. These sponsors play a significant role during the wedding ceremony and throughout the couple’s life.

Step 5: Plan the Wedding Ceremony
Work with the parish priest to plan the details of the wedding ceremony. This includes choosing the hymns, readings, and understanding the rites and customs that will be performed. Ensure you understand the roles and responsibilities during the ceremony.

Step 6: Prepare the Wedding Attire and Items
The bride and groom should prepare their wedding attire, typically traditional Ethiopian clothing. Additionally, prepare the necessary items for the ceremony, such as rings, candles, and the crowns used during the crowning ritual.

Step 7: Attend the Rehearsal
Attend a rehearsal, if possible, to familiarize everyone involved with the ceremony's flow. This helps ensure that the wedding day runs smoothly and that all participants know their roles.

Step 8: Celebrate the Wedding
On the wedding day, arrive at the church with all prepared items and attire. The ceremony will include prayers, hymns, the exchange of vows and rings, and the crowning. The priest will guide the couple through the sacred rituals, uniting them in holy matrimony.

Step 9: Post-Wedding Registration
After the wedding, ensure that the marriage is registered with the church and civil authorities. This may involve submitting a copy of the marriage certificate to the relevant offices for legal recognition.

Step 10: Participate in Post-Wedding Traditions
Engage in the post-wedding traditions and blessings, such as attending a special service or receiving communion as a couple. These traditions help integrate the newlyweds into the community and bless their new life together.
